# Quality Rubric

## Source Grades

- **A**: primary documents/data, peer-reviewed research, governments, official standards.
- **B**: universities, research institutes, academic publishers, rigorous professional organizations.
- **C**: reliable media, verifiable interviews, professional reference works.
- **D**: ordinary blogs, forums, social posts, promotional pages, unsourced video.

D-grade material can document opinion, lived experience, community discourse, or leads. It cannot alone support a core factual claim.

## Claim Types

- `fact`: directly verifiable.
- `source_view`: explicitly attributed position or recollection.
- `analogy`: structural comparison without claiming mechanism or influence.
- `inference`: synthesis produced by the report.
- `uncertainty`: unresolved absence, conflict, or evidence limit.

## Audit Relationships

- `direct`: the cited context directly entails the statement.
- `synthesis`: cited sources support the premises; the connection is the report's inference.
- `editorial`: practical advice or value judgment whose factual premises may be cited.

Audit result: `supported | partially_supported | unsupported | source_unavailable`.

Search snippets and provider-generated summaries remain discovery material until the original source is read.
